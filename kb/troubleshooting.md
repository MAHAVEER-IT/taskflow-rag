# Troubleshooting

## Overview

This article covers common TaskFlow issues, including login problems, missing emails, upload failures, permission errors, and general support procedures. Most issues can be resolved by checking account details, browser settings, workspace permissions, or subscription limits.

If you are working on an urgent project, collect screenshots and error messages before contacting Support. This helps the support team identify the workspace, project, and action involved.

## Steps

1. Refresh TaskFlow and try the action again.
2. Confirm that your internet connection is stable.
3. Sign out and sign back in.
4. Check whether the issue happens in another supported browser.
5. Clear cached site data for TaskFlow if pages or buttons do not load correctly.
6. Confirm that your email address is verified in **Profile settings > Account**.
7. Ask an Admin to review your role and project access.
8. Check **Workspace settings > Billing** for seat, storage, project, or plan limits.
9. Review TaskFlow notifications or email for related warnings.
10. Contact Support from **Help > Contact support** if the issue continues.

When contacting Support, include your workspace name, project name, task link, browser, approximate time of the issue, and a screenshot of any error message.

## Important Notes

- Login issues are often caused by using the wrong email address, expired passwords, or SSO requirements.
- Missing emails may be filtered by spam, junk, promotions, or company quarantine systems.
- Upload failures can happen when files exceed size limits, use unsupported formats, or when storage limits are reached.
- Permission errors usually mean your role or project access does not allow the action.
- Admins can resolve many workspace access, billing, and member management issues.

## Troubleshooting

For login issues, confirm that you are using the correct email address. If needed, use **Forgot password** or select **Continue with SSO** if your company requires single sign-on.

For missing emails, search your inbox for TaskFlow, check spam folders, and ask your IT team to allow `notifications@taskflow.example`.

For upload failures, check the file size, file type, and workspace storage limit. Try uploading a smaller file to confirm whether the issue is file-specific.

For permission errors, ask an Admin or project owner to confirm your role and project membership.

## FAQ

**How do I contact TaskFlow Support?**  
Open **Help > Contact support** in TaskFlow and submit the support form.

**What information should I include in a support request?**  
Include your workspace name, affected project, task link, browser, steps you tried, and the exact error message.

**Why can my teammate do something I cannot?**  
They may have a different role, project membership, or subscription-level permission.

**Should I create a new account to fix access problems?**  
No. Creating a second account can make access harder to resolve. Ask an Admin or Support to review your existing account.
