# Reset Password

## Overview

If you forget your TaskFlow password or need to update it for security reasons, you can request a password reset from the sign-in page. TaskFlow sends a secure reset link to the email address on your account. The link verifies that you have access to the inbox before allowing a new password.

Password reset links are time-limited. For the safest experience, request a new link when you are ready to complete the reset and avoid forwarding the email to anyone else.

## Steps

1. Go to the TaskFlow sign-in page.
2. Select **Forgot password?**.
3. Enter the email address associated with your TaskFlow account.
4. Select **Send reset link**.
5. Open the password reset email from TaskFlow.
6. Select **Reset password**.
7. Enter a new password that meets the password requirements shown on screen.
8. Confirm the new password.
9. Select **Update password**.
10. Return to the sign-in page and sign in with the new password.

If your organization uses single sign-on, TaskFlow may direct you to reset your password through your identity provider instead. In that case, follow your company’s SSO password process.

## Important Notes

- Password reset links expire after 60 minutes.
- A reset link can only be used once.
- Use a strong password with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
- Do not reuse passwords from email, banking, or other work tools.
- If you suspect someone else accessed your account, reset your password and contact your workspace Admin.
- Changing your password may sign you out of other active browser sessions.

## Troubleshooting

If the reset email does not arrive, check your spam, junk, promotions, or quarantine folder. Search for `password reset` and `TaskFlow`. If your company filters external email, ask your IT team to allow messages from `notifications@taskflow.example`.

If the reset link says it is expired or invalid, return to the sign-in page and request a new reset link. Always use the newest email because older links stop working after a newer request is created.

If TaskFlow says no account exists for your email address, confirm that you entered the same email used for your workspace invitation. You may have signed up with a personal email or an email alias.

## FAQ

**Can an Admin reset my password for me?**  
No. Admins can invite, remove, and manage user roles, but they cannot see or set your password.

**What if I no longer have access to my email?**  
Contact your workspace Admin or TaskFlow Support. You may need to verify ownership before account access can be changed.

**Why did I receive a password reset email I did not request?**  
Someone may have entered your email by mistake. Your account remains secure unless the link is used from your inbox.

**Can I reset my password from the mobile browser?**  
Yes. The reset process works on supported desktop and mobile browsers.
